First concepts [ < General < Information and... ]

The very first concepts on a course of cyber security are these two, cyber and security. Read about cyber in the document Maps & Lists. Many kinds of cyber attacks are discussed there. The distractors, of the first MCQ below, i.e. the wrong answer options, are common but not necessary characteristics of cyber attacks. A contribution by "Aify" on this page challenged the earlier correct answer (see below).

Note that every MCQ, as explained in the course handbook, has one correct answer and three distractors. The purpose of distractors - while you are learning - is not to distract but to guide you away from misconceptions towards the correct answer. To make this a little more than mere memorizing, some MCQs may appear a little tricky. You can think of them as conceptual minigames.

One of the easiest ways to carry out any kind of attack is to break something. In the cyber world this can lead to malfunctioning of software in critical systems. That means breaking the integrity of those systems as well. Even if they remained operational they may not be able to fulfill their tasks, which means a lack of availability. An attack with this effect is called denial of service, DoS. And if its method, more commonly called the attack vector, is such that the attack "arrays" point to the target from many directions, the DoS is a distributed DoS. The distractors of the MCQ about DDoS are worth noting because they are characteristics of other attacks.

Finally, what is security, the second and even more profound concept in the course name? Obviously it deserves a deeper definition than this paragraph, and you should study then NIST-Intro mentioned in the Maps & Lists. Put simply, your information is secure when it enjoys confidentiality, integrity and availability, each at the level desired by you. The latter two goals were already mentioned above. Confidentiality appears in one of the MCQs in comparison to the other two.
