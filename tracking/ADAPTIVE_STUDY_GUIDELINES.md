# Adaptive Study Guidelines

Purpose: keep the Cyber Security 1 crash plan realistic, emotionally sustainable, and grade-5 oriented.

## Core Rule

Plans are not fixed promises. They are working hypotheses. At the end of each study block, day, week, and phase, update the next plan based on actual execution.

## What To Track Every Day

Record these in `tracking/PROGRESS_LOG.md` or the daily review file:

- Planned hours vs actual hours.
- Planned tasks vs completed tasks.
- Maso pages completed, with page ids.
- MCQ questions answered with reasoning.
- Harpo exercises prepared, attempted, scores, and attempt risk.
- News tweets submitted.
- Exam 3 output: big essay, small essay, outline, or recall.
- Energy level: high / medium / low.
- Fear/friction level: low / medium / high.
- Biggest blocker.
- One thing that became clearer today.

## Adjustment Rules

### If completion is 80% or more

- Keep next day's plan mostly unchanged.
- Add one small stretch task only if energy is medium/high.
- Do not expand Harpo submissions unless preparation is strong.

### If completion is 50-79%

- Keep the same main goal, but reduce task count by 20-30%.
- Move lower-value tasks to buffer.
- Prioritize Maso + MCQ reasoning before Harpo scoring attempts.

### If completion is below 50%

- Do not punish the next day with a larger plan.
- Find the cause: time, fatigue, confusion, fear, tooling, or overambitious planning.
- Next day plan must have only one main learning objective and one small scoring/support task.

### If errors cluster around one concept

- Stop adding new pages for one block.
- Create a concept repair note:
  - wrong idea
  - correct idea
  - example
  - how to recognize it in MCQ wording

### If Harpo risk is unclear

- Do not submit.
- Read related Maso/Maps material.
- Prepare answers offline.
- Submit only when the checklist is green.

### If fear/friction is high

- Start with a 20-minute concrete task, not a broad reading task.
- Use MCQ extraction or one Maso page as the entry point.
- End with a visible output file, even if small.

## Feedback Style

Every review should include:

- What went well and deserves credit.
- What needs attention.
- What to change in the next plan.
- One concrete next action.

Feedback must be specific. Do not say "study harder". Say which page, concept, answer style, or workflow needs work.

## Grade 5 Priority Order

When time is tight, prioritize:

1. Exam 1 Maso pages and MCQ reasoning.
2. Exam 2 preparation, especially application pages and sample questions.
3. Exam 3 big essay and small essay answer frames.
4. Harpo high-confidence automatic exercises.
5. News daily continuity.
6. Contributions and special exercises when they do not threaten exam prep.

## Weekly Review Questions

- Are we still on track for Exam 1 on 2026-07-27 evening?
- Are Maso pages being understood, or only collected?
- Are MCQ explanations improving?
- Are Harpo attempts being protected?
- Is Exam 3 getting daily or near-daily attention?
- Is the plan producing confidence, or only pressure?
